Line Filters


Line Filters are essential components in compressed air systems used to remove contaminants such as dust, oil, water droplets, and other impurities. They ensure clean, dry, and high-quality compressed air, protecting downstream equipment and improving overall system efficiency. Line filters are widely used in industries where air purity is critical.

Working Principle:

  • Compressed air enters the line filter through the inlet.
  • Larger particles and liquid droplets are removed by a mechanical separation process.
  • The air then passes through a filter element that traps fine dust, oil mist, and micro-particles.
  • Coalescing filters merge small oil and water droplets into larger ones, which are then drained out.
  • The purified air exits through the outlet, ensuring clean and contamination-free airflow.
